Stmikes

Clerical employees at St. Michael's Hospital

Providence, St Joseph’s and St Michael’s merged as of August 1, 2017. The reason non-union employees at St. Michael's are joining CUPE is because now more than ever job security for non-union positions will be tenuous.

On the same day the merger took place, the employer notified employees that all employees will be able to apply for jobs at all locations. For CUPE members, access to CUPE jobs are protected and our members have the first option for those jobs including our CUPE clerical members. Unfortunately for non-union employees, this is not the case. Employees from any location, union or non-union, will be able to bid on non-union jobs at all three locations including St. Michael’s.

Hospitals merge in order to streamline services and costs, and clerical and paramedical positions are typically the first to see restructuring and downsizing. CUPE has the strongest job security language, which includes the longest notice of layoff of any healthcare union, retirement packages and chain bumping into other position. With CUPE’s strong contract language, even though someone may be laid off from their current position it is rare for anyone to be without a job at the end of the restructuring.

Additional Information

Why is it an advantage to be in a Union?

Hospital workers who are CUPE members have a huge advantage over non-union workers because CUPE members are protected by a strong Collective Agreement. When you join a Union you negotiate a Collective Agreement that sets out such things as: Vacation Entitlement (unreduced percentage in lieu or time off, increased with length of service); Rates of Pay and regular procession through pay grids; Fair Shift Scheduling Process & schedules posted in advance; Job Security Language including due to technological change; Layoff & Recall Rights; Health & Safety; and Discipline & Termination for Just Cause only When you have a Collective Agreement the employer must abide by the rights and benefits set out in the Contract and must follow any and all procedures set out there as well.

Clerical News - July

Bargaining | Violence against clerical staff | Technological change

Technological changes pose particular issues for clerical staff Digitization of medical records Automation of dicta-typing and medical transcription Electronic patient registration kiosks These are just some examples of the revolution through technological change taking place in hospitals across Ontario, which is eliminating the jobs of clerical employees. In our central bargaining with Ontario hospitals, CUPE is seeking increased protections against the impact of technological changes on clerical staff.
